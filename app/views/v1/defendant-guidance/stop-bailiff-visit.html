Stop a bailiff visit

A bailiff will contact you 7 days before they come to your home or business. If you can, pay the debt in full or try to set up a payment plan with the bailiff in those 7 days. If you owe council tax, contact the council to set up a payment plan.

If you cannot pay the money or think you do not owe the money, you can ask the court to:

  • suspend a warrant of control
  • stay a writ of control
  • set aside a judgment

You will have to pay a court fee to make your application unless you’re eligible for help with court fees.

If you want to dispute a council tax liability order you must contact your local council. If you do not agree with the council’s decision you can make an appeal to the Valuation Tribunal.

Find out what a bailiff can and cannot do when they visit your home.

Apply to suspend a warrant of control

You can ask the county court to suspend a warrant of control by filling in form n245. The application costs £55.

The court will send a letter to the claimant giving them 14 days to reply to your request. There are 3 possible outcomes:

  • the claimant agrees to suspend the warrant of control
  • the claimant does not reply and the court rules in your favour
  • the claimant does not agree

If the claimant does not agree to suspend the warrant you will have to go to a hearing. At the hearing a district judge will listen to the case on both sides and make a decision.

Apply to stay a writ of control

You can ask the court to stay a writ of control by filling in form n244. The application costs £50. You must make your application at either a district registry or the High Court.

The court will create an urgent hearing for your case meaning you will have to go to court at short notice. If you apply in person you can get your hearing date on the same day, otherwise it will be sent by post.
