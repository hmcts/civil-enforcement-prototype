Dear {{ defendant }},

The High Court has issued a Writ of Control against you.

A Writ of Control is an order from the court to a High Court bailiff, known as a High Court Enforcement Officer (HCEO). It gives an HCEO the right to take goods from your property to pay off your debt.

HCEOs charge fees for their services, which they add to the total debt. This means you are responsible for paying the HCEO fees.

Next steps

The HCEO will contact you before coming to your property. It's likely that they will give you the opportunity to set up a payment plan before their visit. If you can afford to, set up a payment plan when they first contact you.

Stop a Writ of Control

You can apply to stop a Writ of Control, which will stop the HCEO from visiting you.

You can apply to stop a Writ of Control if:

  1. you do not owe the money and want to cancel the county court judgment (CCJ) against you
  2. your financial situation has changed and you want to vary the CCJ

You will need to give evidence to support your application.
